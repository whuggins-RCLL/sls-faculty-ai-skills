# AI assessment decision tree

## AI prohibited
Clarify whether the ban covers all generative AI; whether grammar, accessibility, transcription, citation-management, or private study tools differ; incidental disclosure rules; and whether institutionally approved enforcement language has been supplied. Design the prohibition to be understandable and pedagogically justified.

## AI permitted for limited stages
Identify permitted and prohibited stages, prompt/output retention, disclosure, verification duties, and whether AI use changes what is measured. Possible stages include brainstorming, outlining, editing, counterargument testing, practice questions, research leads, formatting, translation, and accessibility support.

## AI intentionally incorporated
Include learning purpose, access assumptions, alternatives for students without access, data and confidentiality warnings, verification requirements, disclosure, evaluation criteria, debrief or reflection, faculty preparation, and failure modes. Designs may critique AI analysis, verify citations, compare reasoning, revise flawed drafts, document prompts, assess bias, or compare tools.

## Faculty undecided
Ask whether AI would obscure evidence, whether AI literacy is an objective, whether access is equitable, whether disclosed use can be evaluated consistently, whether confidential materials are involved, and whether course policy resolves the question.
