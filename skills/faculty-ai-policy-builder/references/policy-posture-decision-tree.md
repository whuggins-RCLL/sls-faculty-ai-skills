# Policy posture decision tree

Recommend posture only after learning purpose.

## Model 1: AI-restricted
Use when students must demonstrate independent legal reasoning, close reading, foundational research, individual writing development, reliable assessment performance, or protection of exam/client materials. Clarify covered tools/functions; private study; grammar, spelling, accessibility, translation, citation-management; research leads; incidental disclosure; scope; and pedagogical reason.

## Model 2: AI-limited
Use when specific tasks are pedagogically acceptable: brainstorming, questions, counterarguments, study aids, clarity edits, formatting, translation, research leads, or organization comparisons. Specify stages, prohibited stages, verification, disclosure, prompt/output retention, generated language limits, and upload restrictions.

## Model 3: AI-flexible with disclosure
Use when broad use fits goals but needs structure: meaningful disclosure, verification, student responsibility, confidentiality, process evidence, and assignment limits. Do not make this “anything goes.”

## Model 4: AI-infused
Use when AI is deliberately part of learning. Distinguish required, optional, and no-AI activities; alternatives; verification; reflection; documentation; confidentiality; and assessment criteria.

Always include alternatives considered and unresolved decisions.
