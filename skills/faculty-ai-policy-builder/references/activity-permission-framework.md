# Activity permission framework

Decide by activity, not by slogans.

| Activity | Key policy question |
|---|---|
| Brainstorming | May AI generate ideas before the student selects and develops them? |
| Outlining | Must the organization and analysis be the student’s own? |
| Legal research | May AI suggest search terms or authorities, and how must they be verified? |
| Summarization | Would AI summaries undermine close reading of assigned materials? |
| Drafting | May generated language appear in submitted work? |
| Editing | Is surface editing allowed, and when does it become substantive rewriting? |
| Translation | Is translation allowed and disclosed? |
| Citation checking | May AI assist if every citation is independently checked? |
| Data analysis | What tools and documentation are required? |
| Oral preparation | May students generate questions or rehearse? |
| Examinations | Is any AI use permitted? |
| Group work | Must all members know and approve use? |
| Fictional simulations | Are role materials confidential and barred from upload? |

Explain distinctions: brainstorming/drafting, editing/substantive rewriting, leads/verified research, summarization/close reading, generated content/assisted process, disclosure/citation, permitted/good use, access/upload authorization, private study/submitted work, course/assignment rules.
