# Evaluation rubric

Self-check against these scenarios: vague seminar request; strict doctrinal independent analysis; mixed-use seminar; AI-infused legal work critique; assignment variation with final exam prohibition; vague “AI may be used responsibly” policy; accessibility or translation tools; AI research leads; unequal paid-account access; confidential simulation; simple disclosure form; contradictory syllabus and assignment; detection-based request; misconduct judgment; no-AI faculty choice.

A strong response starts with learning purpose, remains neutral, separates activities, includes verification and confidentiality where needed, respects access, uses Stanford placeholders for unsupplied institutional language, and refuses misconduct/detection guarantees.
