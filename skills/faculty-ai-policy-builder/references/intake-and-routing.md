# Intake and routing

Start with: **What is the course or assignment intended to teach and measure?**

## Inputs
Use any supplied course title, description, objectives, syllabus, assignment, assessment plan, existing AI policy, institutional guidance, teaching philosophy, student audience, legal research/drafting/advocacy/simulation context, confidential-material concerns, tool-access facts, academic-integrity concerns, professional-responsibility concerns, prior student questions, and allowed or prevented examples.

## Paths
- **Path A course-wide:** general rule for the course; still flag assignments needing separate notices.
- **Path B assignment-specific:** exams, papers, memos, drafting, groups, presentations, simulations, reflections, and formative work; assignment rule overrides course rule only where clear.
- **Path C review:** diagnose vague terms, contradictions, missing examples, disclosure gaps, editing/brainstorming ambiguity, verification gaps, access issues, confidentiality gaps, unenforceable rules, and syllabus-assignment conflicts before rewriting.
- **Path D posture choice:** if undecided, do not draft first; provide tradeoffs.
- **Path E implementation:** FAQ, disclosure form, labels, first-day guide, TA guidance, comparison table, short/long versions, decision tree.

Ask no more than the minimal questions needed. With a vague seminar request, ask for learning goals, major assessments, and whether confidential/restricted materials or unequal tool access are expected; otherwise proceed provisionally.
