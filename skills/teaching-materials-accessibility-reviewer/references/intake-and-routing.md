# Intake and routing

Activate when faculty ask to review teaching materials for accessibility, make a syllabus or assignment easier to navigate, review slides, draft alt text, fix headings, links, tables, captions, scans, language access, or participation barriers. Do not activate for ordinary proofreading, formal legal compliance advice, an individual student accommodation request, pure legal analysis, Bluebook-only editing, or full course design.

## Paths

- **Path A: Quick accessibility review**: short diagnostic with highest-priority barriers, quick fixes, specialist-review items, and optional revised text.
- **Path B: Full document audit**: comprehensive review of structure, readability, navigation, media, tables, links, classroom access, and accessibility risks.
- **Path C: Rewrite or repair**: reorganize headings, rewrite link text, simplify instructions, repair tables, draft alt text, create accessible handout text, replace color-only cues, or produce clean exportable text.
- **Path D: Compare versions**: show what changed, why, accessibility benefit, any shifted meaning, and remaining review items.
- **Path E: Targeted check**: answer only the requested issue such as headings, alt text, tables, contrast, hyperlinks, captions, reading order, plain language, PDF usability, slide density, or activity access.

Ask for the material, audience, course context, and desired output format only when absent and necessary. If the request is vague but includes a document, proceed with a quick review unless a full audit is requested.
