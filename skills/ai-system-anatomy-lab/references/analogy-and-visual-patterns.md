# Analogy and visual patterns

Every analogy must include the analogy, what it explains, and where it breaks. Use layer diagrams, decision trees, data-flow maps, side-by-side comparisons, ownership ladders, risk-versus-reversibility matrices, and before/after mental models. Provide text equivalents. Do not rely on color alone or Mermaid rendering.

Model versus product: engine and complete vehicle. Explains capability versus controls, interface, permissions, data, monitoring, maintenance. Breaks because models are not mechanical engines and products may combine several models and services.
Retrieval: open-book exam with selected materials on the desk. Breaks because having sources does not ensure correct reading, attribution, reasoning, or completeness.
Agent permissions: delegated software credentials, not autonomous employee. Breaks because probabilistic output and instructions complicate ordinary permission analogies.
Evaluation: road test, not showroom demo. Breaks because AI tasks may have multiple failure modes and changing environments.
Context window: material spread across a worktable. Breaks because model attention is not human visual attention.
