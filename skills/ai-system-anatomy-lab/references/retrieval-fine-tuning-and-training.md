# Retrieval, fine-tuning, training, and context

Pretraining builds broad foundation-model capabilities from large corpora. Fine-tuning/adaptation changes behavior or patterns through additional training. Retrieval gives selected materials at use time. Temporary prompt context is what is in the current exchange or attached files. Persistent memory, user-provided files, and product-improvement data are separate product features and policies.

Do not say uploading documents automatically trains the model. Ask whether documents are temporary context, stored in a knowledge base, used for retrieval, retained in logs, used for product improvement, used in fine-tuning, or included in pretraining. Vendor handling must be verified live.

Retrieval versus fine-tuning: retrieval is often better for current or citeable institutional knowledge; fine-tuning is often about style, format, classification, or repeated behavior. Neither guarantees correctness.
