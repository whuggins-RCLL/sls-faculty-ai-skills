# Ownership and control ladder

When someone says “our own AI” or “our own LLM,” ask: “When you say ‘our own,’ which part do you want the institution to own or control?”

1. Branded interface: owns look and access point; provider supplies model/product; low technical operations; solves recognition and routing.
2. Instructions and faculty workflows: controls prompts, steps, review points; provider supplies platform/model; needs learning design and maintenance.
3. Skills: owns packaged guidance; provider supplies runtime; needs versioning and evaluation.
4. Curated knowledge collection: owns selected documents; provider supplies search/retrieval/model; needs source governance.
5. Retrieval configuration: controls chunking, indexing, access, ranking; provider supplies components; needs testing for omission and citation errors.
6. Permissions, governance, evaluation: controls who can do what and how quality is tested; needs policy and operational ownership.
7. Application using a third-party model: owns app logic; provider supplies model/API/hosting terms; needs engineering, security, monitoring.
8. Hosted open-weight model: controls deployment environment and some data paths; model weights/license come from others; needs ML operations, security, hardware/cloud capacity.
9. Adapted/fine-tuned model: controls task-specific adaptation; base model and training stack may come from others; needs datasets, evaluation, lifecycle management.
10. Foundation model pretrained from the ground up: controls the base model project; requires large-scale data, compute, ML expertise, safety, evaluation, energy, and long-term operations.

Do not say a law school categorically cannot do any level. Explain that requirements vary radically and technical feasibility does not establish institutional usefulness. Quantitative claims require current web research.
