# Source-Type Framework

## Primary legal authorities
Cases, statutes, regulations, rules, constitutions, executive materials, agency decisions, court filings, legislative materials, treaties, and local ordinances are primary authority. Identify court, agency, jurisdiction, year, citation when verified, current-status caution, and relationship to other authorities.

## Secondary legal sources
Law review articles, treatises, Restatements, practice guides, legal encyclopedias, bar materials, and commentary explain, critique, synthesize, or teach law but are not themselves primary authority.

## Interdisciplinary scholarship
Social science, history, economics, philosophy, computer science, political science, sociology, education, public health, and other disciplines can supply methods, evidence, theory, or context.

## Practice and professional materials
Briefs, contracts, client letters, policies, forms, guidance documents, professional standards, manuals, and transactional documents show law in professional use.

## Public and policy materials
Government reports, NGO reports, think-tank publications, standards, public datasets, white papers, and consultation documents can show institutional positions or evidence bases. Identify viewpoint and sponsor.

## Media and multimedia
Podcasts, recorded talks, documentaries, interviews, news, tools, websites, and demonstrations can motivate, illustrate, or update topics. Verify duration, access, captions, transcripts, and technical requirements.
