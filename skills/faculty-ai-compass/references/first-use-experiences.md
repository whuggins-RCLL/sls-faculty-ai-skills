# First-use experiences

Immediate starting experiences that produce value before a full destination Skill.

## 1. What is inside the box?

Ask faculty to identify which parts of an AI product they believe the school would own or control.

**Route:** SLS AI System Anatomy Lab.

**Micro-prompt:** "When you imagine the school 'owning' this assistant, which layers do you think we control: interface, instructions, retrieval index, logs, model weights, hosting?"

## 2. The citation check

Present a short supplied answer with citations. Ask what must be verified before relying on it.

**Route:** Claims Cross-Examiner or Capability and Failure Lab.

**Note:** Use synthetic or public examples only.

## 3. Tool-first reset

Present: "We should use an AI assistant to improve this."

Ask: "What is the actual problem?"

**Route:** Problem-to-AI Use Case Studio.

## 4. Demonstration versus evidence

Ask: "A demonstration succeeds once. What decision does that permit?"

**Route:** Pilot and Evaluation Designer.

## 5. Meaningful human review

Ask: "A faculty member will review everything. What must be true for that review to be meaningful?"

**Route:** Governance and Human Judgment Simulator.

## 6. Teaching transfer

Ask: "What should students be able to do without AI before they use it?"

**Route:** Teaching Transfer Studio.

## 7. Product x-ray

Ask for a product name or URL.

**Route:** Product Decoder.

**Note:** Trigger current web research for product facts.

## 8. Failure observation

Offer a short missing-source or silent-omission thought experiment.

**Route:** Capability and Failure Lab.

## Selection rule

Choose the experience that matches the faculty member's stated need. One experience per opening unless they request more.
