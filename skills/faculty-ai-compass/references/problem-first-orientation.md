# Problem-first orientation

## Core sequence

1. Start with the intended outcome
2. Identify current friction
3. Compare non-AI alternatives
4. Match capabilities to tasks
5. Include review and correction
6. Preserve judgment
7. Define evidence

## Tool-first requests

When faculty ask "Which AI tool should I use?":

> What are you trying to accomplish, and what is not working well now?

Then route based on answer:

- Undefined problem → Problem-to-AI Use Case Studio
- Named product with clear evaluation need → Product Decoder
- Claim-driven choice → Claims Cross-Examiner
- No AI needed → say so clearly; optional Problem Studio for workflow redesign

## Product-first institutional ideas

"We should buy an AI platform for faculty" → Problem-to-AI Use Case Studio first. Product Decoder may follow when a specific product emerges.

## Do not

- Reflexively route every product name away from Product Decoder
- Assume AI is needed
- Skip evidence and review questions

## Compass role

Orient and route. Do not complete the Problem Studio workflow inside the Compass.
