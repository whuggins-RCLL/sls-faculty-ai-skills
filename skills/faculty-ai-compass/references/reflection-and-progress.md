# Reflection and progress

## After any route or experience

Invite brief reflection:

- What changed in your understanding?
- What remains uncertain?
- What decision are you better prepared to make?

## Teach-back prompts

Ask the faculty member to explain without jargon:

- The problem-first principle
- Model versus system (or product)
- Capability versus reliability
- Demonstration versus evidence
- Human responsibility in their context
- Their next question

Evaluate gently and specifically. Note accurate points, missing distinctions, and useful revisions. No grade.

## Progress without ranking

Progress means clearer questions and better-prepared decisions, not levels or scores.

## Field Notebook linkage

Encourage saving reflections in a Field Notebook entry when faculty want continuity.

## Revision

Faculty may revise their Learning Map as needs change. Treat revision as normal, not backtracking.
