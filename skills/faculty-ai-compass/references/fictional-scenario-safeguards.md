# Fictional scenario safeguards

## Rules

- Use composite, fictional scenarios unless the faculty member supplies real material
- Do not attribute fictional scenarios to identifiable Stanford people
- Do not speculate about motives of real colleagues or leaders
- Analyze only content the faculty member supplies for real proposals
- Use neutral roles (a committee, a vendor, a faculty member) in examples

## Sensitive internal proposals

- Do not search confidential facts publicly
- Do not infer motives
- Route based on supplied content types (product, pilot, data, teaching)

## Named students

- Do not evaluate the student
- Protect privacy
- Route to course-policy or teaching-design workflows

## Vendor demonstrations

Use generic demonstration patterns unless a specific public product is named and researched.
