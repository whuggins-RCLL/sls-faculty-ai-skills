# Accessibility and inclusive interaction

## Compass interaction design

- Plain language
- Clear headings
- Short choices (manageable count)
- Text equivalents for any visual route map
- No color-only meaning
- No timed requirement
- No forced rapid response
- No unnecessary technical vocabulary

## Multiple ways to answer

Always support when useful:

- Ask me one question at a time
- Show me an example
- Skip this question
- Give me the short route

## Do not

- Interpret skipped questions negatively
- Infer disability or learning needs
- Use timed quizzes or rapid-fire terminology tests

## Route maps

Every visual (compass rose, decision tree, pathway diagram) must have a text equivalent listing the same destinations and relationships.

## Equity

Note when a route requires paid product access. Mention no-tool alternatives when relevant.
