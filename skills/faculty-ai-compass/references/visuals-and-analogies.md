# Visuals and analogies

Use visuals only when they aid routing. Every visual must have a text equivalent.

## Supported formats

- Compass rose (eight destinations around a center)
- Route map
- Decision tree
- Eight-destination pathway
- Learning journey
- Question-to-Skill map
- Time-and-depth path
- Before-and-after mental model
- Field Notebook progression
- Problem-to-evidence pathway

## Text equivalent example (eight destinations)

```
Center: Your question today
Spokes:
  1. System Anatomy — what is the system made of?
  2. Product Decoder — what is this product?
  3. Claims Cross-Examiner — what does evidence establish?
  4. Problem-to-AI Studio — is AI appropriate for this problem?
  5. Capability and Failure Lab — what happens when we test?
  6. Pilot Designer — does the workflow perform acceptably?
  7. Governance Simulator — who has authority and stopping power?
  8. Teaching Transfer Studio — how should this affect teaching?
```

## Analogies

### Campus map

**Analogy:** The Compass is like a campus map that helps you find the building relevant to your purpose; it does not require visiting every building.

**What it explains:** Different faculty questions belong in different learning experiences.

**Where it breaks:** AI questions may overlap, and the most useful route may change as new evidence appears.

### Medical triage without diagnosis

**Analogy:** The Compass identifies the most useful next form of inquiry rather than resolving every issue immediately.

**What it explains:** A short conversation can determine whether the next step is explanation, evidence review, experimentation, evaluation, governance, or teaching design.

**Where it breaks:** Faculty learning is not medical treatment, and the Compass must not imply deficiency or pathology.

### Trail system

**Analogy:** Faculty may choose a short loop, one destination, or a longer connected path.

**What it explains:** Depth and sequence should match the question and available time.

**Where it breaks:** Learning does not always proceed linearly; returning to an earlier question may represent progress.

### Instrument panel

**Analogy:** The Compass helps identify which indicator deserves attention before a decision.

**What it explains:** Not every concept, risk, or product feature matters equally in every situation.

**Where it breaks:** AI decisions involve values, judgment, and affected people, not only technical signals.

## Do not

- Rely on color alone
- Use decorative complexity instead of clear routing
