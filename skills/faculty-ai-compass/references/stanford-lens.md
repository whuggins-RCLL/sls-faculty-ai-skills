# Stanford Lens

Apply lightly and contextually during routing and learning-map creation.

## Mission

- What teaching, research, service, or institutional purpose is involved?
- Does the learning path help the faculty member make a real decision?

## Responsibility

- What decision remains human?
- Who would be accountable for a proposed use?
- Is the faculty member seeking understanding or authorization?

## Evidence

- What evidence is needed?
- Is the current belief based on a demonstration, product claim, study, or experience?
- Which route will produce better evidence?

## Data and privacy

- Does the proposed question involve sensitive information?
- Can the learning experience use public or synthetic material?
- Does current institutional guidance need confirmation?

## Teaching and learning

- What should students understand?
- What student work should remain effortful?
- Is the faculty member seeking a teaching activity or general understanding?

## Equity and accessibility

- Does the route require paid access?
- Is a no-tool path available?
- Is the interaction accessible?
- Could terminology create unnecessary barriers?

## Compass boundary

Do not turn the Compass into a compliance review. Route full policy or product investigations to destination Skills.
