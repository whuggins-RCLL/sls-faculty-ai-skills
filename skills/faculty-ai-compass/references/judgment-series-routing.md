# Judgment Series routing

Self-contained routing boundaries for all eight destination Skills. Recommend one primary route whenever possible.

## 1. SLS AI System Anatomy Lab

**Faculty question:** What is an AI system actually made of?

**Route when:** Pure conceptual confusion; layers (model, interface, retrieval, agent, workflow, permissions); "our own LLM"; training vs context vs retrieval; hosting and ownership questions without a named vendor comparison focus.

**Boundary:** Not full product due diligence, course policy drafting, or substantive legal research.

**Opening prompt example:** "Help me map the layers of this proposed AI assistant and clarify what 'our own' would mean for each layer."

**Artifact:** System layer map, ownership ladder, misconception reset.

## 2. SLS AI Product Decoder

**Faculty question:** What is this current product, feature, model, or vendor, and what additional value does it provide?

**Route when:** Named product or URL; vendor demo preparation; architecture and differentiation questions; Stanford public service status for a specific tool.

**Boundary:** Not a substitute for claims examination when the primary issue is evidence behind a headline; not problem framing when no product is named yet.

**Precursor:** System Anatomy Lab only when the faculty member cannot interpret product categories or layers.

**Opening prompt example:** "Decode [product name] for a faculty audience: what it is, what powers it, what is differentiated, and what I should ask in a demonstration."

**Artifact:** Product briefing, demo question list, safe narrow trial suggestion.

## 3. SLS AI Claims Cross-Examiner

**Faculty question:** What exactly is being claimed, and what does the evidence establish?

**Route when:** Headlines, vendor claims, benchmarks, studies, predictions, legal or productivity claims, "AI is better than lawyers."

**Boundary:** Not full product architecture review unless claim requires it; do not conduct entire examination in Compass.

**Opening prompt example:** "Examine this claim: [paste claim]. What does the evidence actually establish, and what would I need to test?"

**Artifact:** Claim anatomy, evidence audit, calibrated finding.

## 4. SLS Problem-to-AI Use Case Studio

**Faculty question:** What problem are we trying to solve, and is AI an appropriate part of the solution?

**Route when:** Recurring frustration; teaching bottleneck; research workflow pain; tool-first ideas ("we should buy an AI platform"); chatbot proposed before problem defined.

**Boundary:** Not product comparison when problem is undefined; may recommend no AI.

**Opening prompt example:** "Here is the friction we are trying to solve: [describe]. Help me decide whether AI belongs in the solution and what a non-AI alternative would look like."

**Artifact:** Problem brief, use-case map, non-AI alternatives.

## 5. SLS AI Capability and Failure Lab

**Faculty question:** What happens when we test the behavior directly?

**Route when:** Desire to observe hallucination, citation errors, omissions, variability, sycophancy; classroom experiment; "show me why I cannot trust citations."

**Boundary:** Not structured pilot design when use case and baseline already exist.

**Opening prompt example:** "Design a five-minute safe experiment that reveals whether this system admits missing sources."

**Artifact:** Lab record, failure interpretation, next test.

## 6. SLS AI Pilot and Evaluation Designer

**Faculty question:** Does the complete workflow perform acceptably under representative conditions?

**Route when:** Defined use case, candidate system, baseline, representative tasks, reviewers, desire for structured pilot.

**Boundary:** Not basic orientation; not governance authority decisions alone.

**Opening prompt example:** "We have [use case] and [product]. Help me design a pilot with success criteria, tasks, and review burden."

**Artifact:** Pilot plan, evaluation matrix, success criteria.

## 7. SLS AI Governance and Human Judgment Simulator

**Faculty question:** Who has authority, responsibility, access, review duties, correction duties, and stopping power?

**Route when:** Permissions, agents, human review, accountability, logging, appeals, institutional decision rights.

**Boundary:** Not product marketing review; does not authorize use.

**Opening prompt example:** "Map who can approve, review, correct, and stop this proposed agent workflow involving [general description]."

**Artifact:** Governance map, meaningful-review checklist, open authority questions.

## 8. SLS AI Teaching Transfer Studio

**Faculty question:** How should this understanding affect teaching and student learning?

**Route when:** Course AI use, assignments, learning objectives, student reasoning visibility, professional judgment in teaching.

**Boundary:** Not full syllabus redesign alone—hand off to Course and Syllabus Designer when that is the deliverable.

**Opening prompt example:** "Should students use AI for [assignment type]? What should they be able to do without AI first?"

**Artifact:** Teaching transfer brief, activity sketch, verification exercise idea.

## Route precedence

| Situation | Primary route | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Specific current product named | Product Decoder | Anatomy only if categories unclear |
| Broad claim or statistic | Claims Cross-Examiner | Not Product Decoder unless claim is mainly about one product |
| Real problem, no AI approach | Problem-to-AI Use Case Studio | Not Product Decoder first |
| Desire to see what AI does | Capability and Failure Lab | |
| Defined use + structured testing | Pilot and Evaluation Designer | |
| Permissions / oversight | Governance Simulator | |
| Course / student learning | Teaching Transfer Studio | Teaching collection for full deliverables |
| Pure conceptual confusion | System Anatomy Lab | |
