# Decision-based diagnostic

Use realistic decisions and situations. Select dimensions selectively; do not ask every question.

## System mental model situations

- A product says it is "trained on our documents." What would you want to clarify?
- A colleague says the school should build its own LLM. Which meaning of "our own" might they intend?
- A system retrieves the correct case but states the rule incorrectly. Which system layer failed?

**Route:** SLS AI System Anatomy Lab when layers, retrieval, training, agents, or hosting are confused.

## Product judgment situations

- A vendor demonstrates one excellent result. What does that establish?
- An agent requests access to an entire shared drive. What questions matter?
- Faculty ask about a named product before a meeting tomorrow.

**Route:** SLS AI Product Decoder when a current product, feature, or vendor is the focus.

## Evidence judgment situations

- A model provides citations. What would you check before relying on them?
- A headline says AI passed the bar exam. What does that establish about legal practice?
- A vendor claims 70% time savings. What evidence would you need?

**Route:** SLS AI Claims Cross-Examiner for claims, benchmarks, studies, predictions.

## Problem framing situations

- "We should use an AI assistant to improve this." What is the actual problem?
- A department proposes buying an AI platform before defining workflows.
- Faculty want to save time but have not described the friction.

**Route:** SLS Problem-to-AI Use Case Studio.

## Direct experience situations

- Faculty want to see why citations cannot simply be trusted.
- Faculty noticed silent omission in an answer.
- Faculty want a classroom-safe failure demonstration.

**Route:** SLS AI Capability and Failure Lab.

## Evaluation readiness situations

- A pilot saves drafting time but doubles review time. How should success be evaluated?
- Faculty have a use case, product, baseline, reviewers, and public test materials.

**Route:** SLS AI Pilot and Evaluation Designer.

## Governance situations

- A professor will review every output. What makes that review meaningful?
- Who can stop an agent workflow? Who is accountable for errors?

**Route:** SLS AI Governance and Human Judgment Simulator.

## Teaching transfer situations

- Should students use AI for a legal research assignment?
- What should students be able to do without AI before they use it?
- Faculty found a subtle omission and want students to examine it.

**Route:** SLS AI Teaching Transfer Studio.

## Diagnostic stop rule

Stop asking when you can name one primary route with a clear reason. Offer the route card and one immediate next step.
