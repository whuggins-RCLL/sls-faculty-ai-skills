# Confidence and calibration

## Purpose

Help faculty distinguish concept uncertainty, product uncertainty, and institutional decision uncertainty. Do not score confidence.

## Questions to use selectively

- How confident are you in that understanding?
- What makes you confident?
- What evidence would change your mind?
- Is your uncertainty about the concept, the product, or the institutional decision?

## Confidence labels (faculty self-report only)

- Low confidence
- Conditional confidence
- Moderate confidence
- High confidence

Do not assign confidence for the faculty member without evidence from the conversation.

## Calibration patterns

### High confidence with thin evidence

Acknowledge the clarity without shaming. Suggest a small test or evidence review:

> You sound clear about the distinction. A short experiment or claim check could confirm whether that clarity holds for your specific use case.

**Route:** Capability and Failure Lab or Claims Cross-Examiner as appropriate.

### Low confidence with accurate reasoning

Recognize accurate reasoning explicitly. Do not route to basics by default:

> Your reasoning already separates capability from appropriate use. The more useful next step may be framing the decision or designing a small pilot.

### Confident misconception

Use misconception reset respectfully. Route to System Anatomy Lab when training/retrieval confusion is central.

### Low-confidence correct understanding

Affirm the accurate part. Offer a short confirmation activity rather than a remedial path.

## Do not

- Treat high confidence as a problem
- Treat low confidence as a deficit
- Use confidence as a routing score
