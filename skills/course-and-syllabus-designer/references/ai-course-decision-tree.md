# AI Course Decision Tree

Do not assume AI integration. Ask faculty to choose or confirm one posture.

## Path 1: AI-restricted

Clarify scope: whole course or selected assignments; treatment of spelling, grammar, transcription, accessibility, and translation tools; private study; research leads; generated text, analysis, outlines, or citations; examinations; confidential materials; disclosure; and how students should ask about uncertainty.

Help explain pedagogical reasons, such as independent legal reasoning, individual assessment, close reading, foundational research skills, confidential assessment materials, and avoiding fabricated authorities.

## Path 2: Selective or mixed AI use

Specify permitted, prohibited, and permission-required uses. Address human verification, disclosure/acknowledgment, prompt/output preservation, assignment-specific rules, and confidential-material limits.

Common permitted uses: brainstorming, generating questions, testing counterarguments, clarity edits, study aids, organizational comparisons, practice hypotheticals, and research leads students independently verify.

Common restricted uses: final analysis, graded writing, unverified citations, uploading confidential material, exams, and replacing independent research.

## Path 3: AI-infused

Define AI literacy outcomes tied to the substantive course: appropriate/inappropriate use, prompting, verification, hallucination/citation risk, bias and representational harm, confidentiality, professional responsibility, tool comparison, human review, disclosure, evaluating AI output, and changes to legal practice or institutions.

Possible activities: structured AI exercises, human/AI analysis comparison, verification assignments, prompt/output critiques, simulations, red-team exercises, reflection memos, disclosed AI-assisted drafting, and evaluation of vendor or court AI uses.

## Required output

Every course package must include one of: a complete draft AI policy, a statement that no separate AI policy is intentionally included, or a placeholder listing unresolved decisions before drafting.

Never state that language is official Stanford policy unless authoritative Stanford language was provided or retrieved and cited.
