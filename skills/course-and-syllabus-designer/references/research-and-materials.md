# Research and Materials

Use web research when faculty request new authorities, current developments, examples from other courses, scholarship, multimedia, datasets, professional guidance, or contemporary problems.

Favor primary and authoritative sources. For legal materials verify existence, jurisdiction, date, holding/relevance, subsequent treatment when material, public accessibility, and instructional fit. Never invent cases, citations, authors, publications, quotations, or holdings.

Label materials as faculty supplied, actually retrieved, web researched, unverified suggestion, or fictional instructional material. External syllabi may inform common sequences and approaches but must not be copied wholesale.
