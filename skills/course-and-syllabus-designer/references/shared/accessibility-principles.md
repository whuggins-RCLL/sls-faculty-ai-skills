# Accessibility Principles

Instructional materials should be designed for accessibility from the beginning.

## Baseline expectations

- Use clear headings, lists, and plain language where appropriate.
- Provide descriptive link text instead of bare URLs or vague labels.
- Include alternative text guidance for meaningful images and mark decorative images as decorative.
- Ensure activities and assessments do not depend on a single sensory mode.
- State expectations, deadlines, participation modes, and grading criteria clearly.
- Consider flexible formats, predictable structure, and compatibility with assistive technologies.

## Review prompts

- Can learners find learning goals, required materials, due dates, and support resources quickly?
- Are instructions actionable and free of avoidable ambiguity?
- Are accommodations and accessibility support channels represented according to institutional policy?
- Are technology requirements and alternatives clearly described?
