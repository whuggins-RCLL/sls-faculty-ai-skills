# Fictional Materials

Create original hypotheticals, fact patterns, client scenarios, simulations, negotiation exercises, draft contracts, mock statutes/regulations, fictional opinions, role-play materials, and problem sets only when requested or useful.

Prominently label every item: `FICTIONAL INSTRUCTIONAL MATERIAL — NOT LEGAL AUTHORITY`. Do not use real-looking reporter, docket, court, or statutory citations. When fictional material incorporates real law, identify the rules or assumptions requiring faculty verification.

Explain the instructional purpose of each original addition, especially in hybrid packages that pair verified sources with fictional variations or simulations.
