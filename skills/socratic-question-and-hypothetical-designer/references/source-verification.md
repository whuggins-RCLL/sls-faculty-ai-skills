# Source verification

For real authorities, verify existence, jurisdiction, and date when the user asks for current law or additional authority. Do not invent quotations or holdings. Distinguish retrieved sources from faculty-provided sources and note when current validity has not been checked.

Fictional material must be labeled: FICTIONAL INSTRUCTIONAL HYPOTHETICAL — not a real case or authority. Do not create fake reporter citations or invented quotations from real people.

For hybrid material, identify the source case, explain changed facts, do not present adaptations as the real dispute, and keep authority separate from instructional variation. If the hypothetical depends on real law, include faculty verification needed for rule, authority, jurisdiction, or procedural assumptions.
