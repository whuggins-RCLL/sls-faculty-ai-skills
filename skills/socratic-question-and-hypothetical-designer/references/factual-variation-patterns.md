# Factual variation patterns

Use a ladder where each variation changes one or two facts and records the analytical effect.

Table columns: Variation, Changed fact, Expected doctrinal effect, Discussion purpose.

Useful patterns: change actor intent, timing, notice, relationship, forum, procedural posture, remedy, statutory text, burden of proof, public/private actor, reliance, foreseeability, authority hierarchy, or institutional role. Avoid changing too many variables at once unless the purpose is synthesis.
