# AI-assisted activities

Ask whether faculty want AI involved. Do not add AI merely because this collection concerns AI skills.

Modes:
- No AI: design without requiring AI.
- AI as object of critique: evaluate AI answers, locate fabricated cases, compare AI reasoning with a court, identify omitted facts or counterarguments.
- AI as student tool: generate initial arguments for critique, produce variations, simulate opposing counsel, or test formulations.

For any AI activity include learning purpose, student instructions, verification requirement, confidentiality warning, no-tool alternative, debrief questions, and limits on submitted AI-generated work.
