# Difficulty ladder

Calibrate cognitive demand and generally move upward.

- Level 1 Locate: find a fact, rule, quotation, or procedural event.
- Level 2 Explain: restate reasoning or doctrine in the student's own words.
- Level 3 Apply: use the rule on changed facts.
- Level 4 Distinguish: explain why one result differs from another.
- Level 5 Evaluate: critique reasoning, policy, fairness, or administrability.
- Level 6 Synthesize: integrate multiple authorities or doctrinal strands.
- Level 7 Exercise judgment: advise, argue, draft, or choose under uncertainty.

When faculty request a demanding sequence, include accessible entry points, diagnostic follow-ups, and recovery questions before high-pressure evaluation.
