# Evaluation rubric

Review purposefulness, progression, difficulty, ambiguity, timing, inclusivity, repetition, doctrinal accuracy, fictional labeling, source status, closing synthesis, and faculty decisions still needed.

A strong output has clear objectives, purpose-tagged questions, an upward difficulty path, constructive branches, realistic hypotheticals, separated student and faculty materials, sensitive-topic review where needed, verification notes, and no invented legal authority.
