# Sensitive-topic guidance

Sensitive topics include violence, sexual assault, discrimination, immigration status, trauma, disability, race, gender identity, religion, poverty, mental health, family conflict, and criminal victimization.

Ask whether the sensitive detail is instructionally necessary. Avoid gratuitous or stereotyped facts, use neutral respectful language, flag foreseeable classroom concerns, offer less sensitive alternatives when the doctrine can be taught without the detail, and avoid identity characteristics as decoration. Preserve legal realism where the topic itself requires difficult facts.
