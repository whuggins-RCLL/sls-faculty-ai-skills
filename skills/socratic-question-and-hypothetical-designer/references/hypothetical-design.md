# Hypothetical design

Before drafting, establish doctrine or analytical distinction, student level, difficulty, time available, one-answer versus multiple-defensible-answer design, and whether the hypothetical will be discussed, written, argued, or assessed.

Every original hypothetical must include a design note with doctrine tested, learning objective, legal distinction, intended difficulty, expected discussion time, facts that matter, distracting facts, intentional ambiguities, likely arguments, and faculty verification needed.

Types: single-variable variation, boundary hypothetical, competing-authority hypothetical, role-based hypothetical, sequential hypothetical, drafting hypothetical, professional-judgment hypothetical, and policy-reversal hypothetical.
