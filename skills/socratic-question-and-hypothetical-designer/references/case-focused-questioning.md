# Case-focused questioning

For a case, identify procedural posture, legally significant facts, governing rule, court reasoning, rejected arguments, unresolved questions, policy consequences, and relationship to other authorities.

Build a progression:
1. Orientation and posture.
2. Fact selection and omitted facts.
3. Rule and source.
4. Reasoning and rejected alternatives.
5. Application through changed facts.
6. Counterarguments and dissenting/alternative reasoning.
7. Policy consequences.
8. Synthesis with prior or next authorities.

When current validity matters, verify sources rather than relying on memory.
