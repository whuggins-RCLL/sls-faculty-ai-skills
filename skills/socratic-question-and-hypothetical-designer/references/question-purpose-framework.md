# Question-purpose framework

Tag questions visibly when useful. Use tags to prevent question lists with no teaching purpose.

1. Orientation: parties, court, posture, relief, chronology, lower court.
2. Fact selection: material, background, irrelevant, majority-emphasized, dissent-emphasized, missing facts.
3. Rule identification: holding, test, factor, presumption, exception, source, mandatory and discretionary parts, breadth.
4. Reasoning: rule-to-result moves, load-bearing premise, rejected alternatives, policy, unstated assumptions.
5. Application: changed fact, new jurisdiction, procedural stage, different party, remedy, borderline scenario.
6. Counterargument: strongest other-side theory, favorable fact, authority, condition needed to prevail.
7. Comparison and synthesis: relation to another authority, consistency, control, principled distinction, broader doctrine.
8. Policy and consequences: incentives, costs, institutional values, administrability, extension effects.
9. Professional judgment: advice, disclosed uncertainty, needed facts, ethical or strategic concern, assumptions to avoid.
10. Reflection and closure: changed understanding, narrowest rule, unresolved issues, bridge to next authority.

Orientation should not consume the discussion. A complete sequence should include synthesis or closure.
