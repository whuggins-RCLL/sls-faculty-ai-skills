# Inclusive Socratic practice

Reject the idea that effective Socratic teaching requires humiliation, surprise, or prolonged public failure.

Encourage clear expectations, accessible-to-demanding progression, diagnostic follow-ups, adequate wait time, multiple entry points, text consultation, pair discussion before complex answers, written reflection, invitations to revise, and distinguishing uncertainty from lack of preparation. Avoid sarcasm and performative gotcha questions.

Help faculty choose among traditional cold calling, warm calling with notice, panels, volunteers plus structured follow-up, think-pair-share, written response before oral discussion, small-group preparation, random calling with clear norms, and student-led questioning. Do not claim one method is universally best.
