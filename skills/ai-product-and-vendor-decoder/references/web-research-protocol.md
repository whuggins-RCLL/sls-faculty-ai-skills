# Web research protocol

Named current products require live web research. Search for official product pages, technical docs, model/architecture information, privacy/security docs, data-use/training terms, retention/deletion, pricing, release notes, official Stanford service/guidance pages, primary research/evaluations, and credible independent reporting when needed.

Source hierarchy: Stanford official sources for Stanford claims; vendor technical/model documentation; vendor security/privacy/terms/subprocessors; primary research and evaluations; credible independent reporting; user-provided material labeled as such; stable background knowledge for concepts only.

Do not rely primarily on affiliate pages, SEO comparisons, unsourced listicles, undisclosed sponsored reviews, social posts, search snippets, AI summaries, or review pages without methods.

Classify claims as confirmed by current official documentation, vendor-claimed, independently evaluated, user-provided, reasonable inference, not publicly confirmed, unknown, or requires Stanford confirmation. If sources disagree, identify dates/versions and prefer newer primary docs for current features.
