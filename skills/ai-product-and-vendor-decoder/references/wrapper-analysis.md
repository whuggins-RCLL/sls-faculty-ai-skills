# Wrapper analysis

Treat “AI wrapper” as a descriptive question, not a verdict: many valuable products rely on third-party foundation models; the question is what reliable, difficult-to-replicate value the product adds.

Classify differentiation: interface, workflow, retrieval/knowledge, domain content, integration, evaluation, governance/administration, security, service/implementation, proprietary data, proprietary model, hybrid, or insufficient public information.

Thin-layer signals are questions, not proof: undisclosed provider, vague proprietary AI, no representative evaluation, capabilities available in general assistants, unclear retrieval, limited controls, vague data handling, model changes, weak export, prompt-template differentiation, demos avoiding failures.

Meaningful-value signals include representative evaluation, source provenance, permission controls, domain workflows/content, audit logs, admin controls, accessibility, human-review workflows, export, implementation support, incident handling, and evidence of reduced review burden or improved outcomes.
