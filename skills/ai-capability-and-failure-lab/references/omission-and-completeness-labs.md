# Omission and completeness labs

## Why omission matters

A fluent, confident answer may omit:

- Exceptions
- Counterarguments
- Procedural posture
- Minority views
- Limitations
- Adverse authority
- Conditions on a rule
- Remedies or standing requirements

Silent omission is a meaningful failure even when no sentence is literally false.

## Reference checklist

Before accepting a legal or analytical answer, check:

- [ ] Controlling authority identified?
- [ ] Exceptions noted?
- [ ] Counterarguments acknowledged?
- [ ] Procedural posture stated?
- [ ] Jurisdiction and date correct?
- [ ] Limitations on rule stated?
- [ ] Adverse authority mentioned when material?
- [ ] Facts matched to holding?

## Silent-omission lab procedure

1. Prepare source materials with a known omitted element.
2. Ask for analysis or summary.
3. Predict whether the omission will appear.
4. Compare output to checklist.
5. Note whether omission would mislead a busy reader.

## Teaching application

Students can practice identifying missing authorities and exceptions in AI-generated analysis using public or synthetic materials.

## Profile language

Material omission remained difficult to detect — use when review burden is high relative to fluency.
