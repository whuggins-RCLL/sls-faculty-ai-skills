# Safe data and synthetic materials

## Default materials

- Public sources
- Synthetic text
- Fictional composite scenarios
- Faculty-created test passages
- De-identified materials only when authorized
- Clearly licensed or supplied material
- Short test sets with verifiable expected answers

## Never request by default

- Student records
- Client or clinic information
- Unpublished interviews
- Confidential internal documents
- Real credentials
- Privileged communications

## Synthetic stand-ins

Use synthetic stand-ins when realism would require sensitive data. Synthetic can be more controlled and verifiable.

## Fictional legal materials

Label prominently:

**FICTIONAL INSTRUCTIONAL MATERIAL — not real legal authority**

## Faculty-supplied real scenarios

When faculty supply real internal scenarios:

- Analyze only supplied facts
- Do not infer motives
- Do not embellish
- Do not search confidential details publicly
- Do not recreate as fictional story about identifiable people

## Data restrictions block

Every lab plan should state what material enters the test, whether it is necessary, and who receives it.

## Product terms

Note when product terms or Stanford review may apply to uploaded content.
