# Web research and freshness

## When live research is required

Use live web research when the lab depends on:

- A named current product
- A named model
- A current feature
- Current tool access
- Current context limits
- Current retrieval behavior
- Current agent permissions
- Current Stanford service information
- A current benchmark or vendor claim

## Record for every current-dependent lab

- Research checked date
- Product
- Model or version when available
- Account tier when relevant
- Enabled tools
- Known configuration
- Source basis

## Source priority

1. Official product documentation
2. Official Stanford sources for Stanford services
3. Primary legal authorities for legal verification
4. Original research and benchmarks
5. Credible independent reporting
6. User-provided material (labeled)

## Limits

Do not promise identical results across dates, accounts, or configurations.

Do not invent model versions, features, or benchmark results.

## No web access

Use stable lab-design concepts. State verification limits. Do not make current product claims from memory alone.

## Handoffs

Full product due diligence → SLS AI Product Decoder.

Broad empirical or marketing claims → SLS AI Claims Cross-Examiner.
