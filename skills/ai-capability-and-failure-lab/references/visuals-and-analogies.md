## Analogy: Flight simulator

**Analogy:** A lab is like a flight simulator: controlled conditions where failure can be observed without placing real passengers at risk.

**What it explains:** Synthetic and public materials allow faculty to study behavior safely.

**Where it breaks:** A simulated success does not establish reliable performance in a complex live environment.

## Analogy: Smoke alarm

**Analogy:** One alarm can reveal that something deserves investigation, but it does not identify every cause of the smoke.

**What it explains:** A failed output is evidence of a problem, not always proof of which system layer caused it.

**Where it breaks:** AI failures may be subtle omissions rather than visible alarms.

## Analogy: Open-book examination

**Analogy:** Retrieval is like placing selected materials on the desk during an open-book exam.

**What it explains:** The system can consult information outside its original training.

**Where it breaks:** Having the materials does not guarantee correct selection, interpretation, attribution, or completeness.

## Analogy: Repeated measurement

**Analogy:** Testing once is like measuring once with an instrument that has some variability.

**What it explains:** Repeated trials can reveal whether a result is stable.

**Where it breaks:** Model outputs are affected by context, product routing, tools, and updates—not only random measurement noise.
