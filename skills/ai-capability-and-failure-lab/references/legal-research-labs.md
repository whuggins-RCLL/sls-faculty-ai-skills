# Legal research labs

Law-faculty-centered experiment patterns. Default to public law, faculty-verified materials, or clearly labeled fiction.

## Pattern library

| Pattern | Setup | What to inspect |
|---------|-------|-----------------|
| Fictional citation | Labeled fictional authority only | Whether system treats as real |
| Real case, changed fact | Public case with one altered fact | Whether analysis tracks the change |
| Rule with omitted exception | Statute or rule excerpt missing exception | Silent omission |
| Quotation out of context | Partial quote from public source | Context restoration |
| Conflicting authorities | Two real sources in tension | Whether tension acknowledged |
| Different jurisdictions | Same issue, two jurisdictions | Jurisdiction confusion |
| Historical vs current law | Superseded and current versions | Temporal confusion |
| Primary vs secondary | Secondary source overstates holding | Authority hierarchy |
| Discusses but does not establish | Source mentions proposition without holding | Overstatement |
| Case summary missing posture | Procedural posture omitted from prompt materials | Procedural omission |
| Research answer, missing key source | Corpus missing controlling authority | Missing-source behavior |
| Source-grounded comparison with omission | Two sources, one limitation hidden | Selective synthesis |
| Hypothetical with irrelevant facts | Distracting facts added | Reasoning focus |
| Persuasive answer on false premise | Incorrect premise in prompt | Premise challenge |

## Fictional authority label

Every fictional legal item must include prominently:

**FICTIONAL INSTRUCTIONAL MATERIAL — not real legal authority**

Never create real-looking fictional citations that could be mistaken for actual authority outside the exercise.

## Example fictional citation format

Use obviously instructional names, e.g., *In re Synthetic Harbor LLC*, FICTIONAL INSTRUCTIONAL MATERIAL — not real legal authority, 2024 WL 000000 (Fict. Ct. 2024).

## Verification requirement

Every legal-research lab requires faculty or reference verification. The Skill does not replace legal research or citator services.

## No citator claims

Do not claim citator-level validation without an actual citator tool.
