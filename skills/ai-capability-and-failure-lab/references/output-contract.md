# Output contract

Modular outputs—produce only what the faculty member needs.

## 1. Five-minute lab card

See lab-record-template. Quick demonstration with debrief.

## 2. Full laboratory plan

Complete test design with learning question, variables, controls, procedure, interpretation, limitations, debrief.

## 3. Output diagnosis

| Possible layer | Evidence | Confidence | Test |

Plus provisional finding and follow-up experiment.

## 4. Capability profile

| Capability | Tested task | Result | Reliability unknowns | Verification | Appropriate use |

Profile language: strong performance on this narrow task; useful with straightforward verification; promising but variable; poor fit for this task. No universal model grade.

## 5. Failure profile

| Failure mode | Observed? | Severity | Detectability | Likely layer | Mitigation |

## 6. Variability comparison

| Element | Trial 1 | Trial 2 | Trial 3 | Meaningful difference |

## 7. Source-support audit

| Proposition | Claimed source | Source exists | Supports claim | Important omission |

## 8. Classroom exercise

Faculty overview, student instructions, materials, no-tool option, timing, accessibility, data rules, debrief, optional assessment, faculty answer notes, what exercise does not prove.

## 9. Vendor-demo lab sheet

Claim being tested; public or synthetic input; expected output; difficult variant; missing-source case; omission case; permission question; evidence to request; decision not to make during demonstration.

## 10. Personal field notebook entry

Prior belief, test, result, revised belief, remaining uncertainty, application, follow-up experiment.

## 11. Human-review burden report

Reviewer, expertise, time, detectable vs hard-to-detect errors, escalation, whether review defeats expected savings.

## 12. Experiment handoff

Concise handoff to planned SLS AI Pilot and Evaluation Designer when broader testing is warranted.

## Formatting principles

Conversational, skimmable, precise, accessible, honest about uncertainty. Use tables when they clarify. Avoid giant essays, hype, fear, gotcha framing, and unsupported certainty.
