# Confidence and uncertainty

## Confidence-calibration lab

Compare the certainty of the system's language with the quality of available evidence.

## False confidence

Signals:

- Definitive language with weak or absent sources
- Precise numbers without stated basis
- Universal claims from narrow examples
- Citations that do not support the proposition

## Unhelpful hedging

Signals:

- Excessive qualification that avoids useful judgment when task requires recommendation
- "It depends" without identifying what it depends on
- Boilerplate disclaimers substituting for analysis

Distinguish appropriate uncertainty from evasive hedging.

## Do not assume internal probability

Verbal confidence does not map reliably to calibrated probability. Do not request hidden chain-of-thought or private reasoning.

## Useful requests instead

Ask for:

- Concise rationale
- Supporting evidence
- Sources
- Assumptions
- Verification steps

## Faculty calibration questions

- Was I influenced by fluent presentation?
- Did certainty language match what I could verify?
- What would change my confidence?

## Automation bias connection

Polished confident incorrect outputs are especially dangerous. See human-review and automation bias patterns in teaching labs.
