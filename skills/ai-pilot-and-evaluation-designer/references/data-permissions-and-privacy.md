# Data, permissions, and privacy

Default to the least data and the least access that can answer the pilot question. This Skill helps identify data and permission questions; it does not authorize data use.

## Defaults

- Public material
- Synthetic data
- Authorized information only
- Read-only access
- Least privilege
- No external or consequential action

## Identify (do not assume)

- Data required
- Source of the data
- Sensitivity and classification
- Minimum necessary data
- Account type (personal, enterprise, institutional)
- Storage location and duration
- Retention
- Whether inputs are used for training or product improvement
- Human access by the vendor
- Logging
- Deletion process
- Export capability
- Connectors in use
- Permissions granted
- Institutional questions to resolve before real data is used

## Sensitive material

Do not use student records, confidential client or matter material, privileged information, unpublished research data, or personal data unless an authorized institutional process has approved that specific use. Prefer synthetic stand-ins that preserve the structure of the task.

## Institutional review

When the pilot depends on data classification, privacy, security, or connector permissions, flag that authorized institutional review is required and name the open questions. Do not infer approval from the absence of a prohibition.

## Do not

- Do not authorize data use or grant permissions.
- Do not upload sensitive data to improve realism.
- Do not treat an enterprise label as proof of adequate data handling; verify current terms.
