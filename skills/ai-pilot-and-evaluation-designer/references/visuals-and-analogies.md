# Visuals and analogies

Use plain-language analogies and simple text visuals to make evaluation concepts concrete for faculty without technical background. Always provide a text equivalent for any diagram.

## Analogies

- **Demonstration vs. pilot:** a cooking-show dish plated under ideal conditions vs. serving the same dish through a full dinner service on a busy night.
- **Baseline:** you cannot say a new route is faster without timing the route you drive now.
- **Critical failure not averaged:** a smoke detector that works 95% of the time is not "an A"; the 5% is the whole point.
- **Omission testing:** a contract review that misses the one clause that matters is not "mostly right."
- **Human-review burden:** if proofreading a draft takes as long as writing it, the draft did not save time.
- **Variability:** asking the same question twice and getting two different legal conclusions is a problem, not a style choice.

## Simple text visual

Evidence ladder (weakest to strongest):

Demonstration -> Capability lab -> Limited pilot -> Larger evaluation -> Deployment

Text equivalent: each step tests more of the real workflow under more representative conditions; a result at one step does not license a claim at a later step.

## Rules

- Keep analogies short and accurate; do not let an analogy overclaim.
- Provide a text description for every visual.
- Prefer one clear analogy over several competing ones.
