# Web research and freshness

Current AI products, models, tiers, benchmarks, vendor policies, and institutional guidance change frequently. When a pilot depends on any current fact, research it live and date the finding.

## When live research is required

- A named current product, model, feature, or tier
- Vendor data-handling, retention, or terms
- Benchmark or public evaluation claims
- Current Stanford services or guidance
- Recent studies or reports referenced in the pilot
- Statements of current law used as reference answers

## Method

- Consult current, authoritative sources; prefer official product documentation and official institutional pages.
- Record what was checked and the research-checked date.
- Distinguish what the source states from inference.
- Note where sources conflict or are silent.

## Freshness note

Every output that relies on current facts includes a short freshness note: what was researched, the date, and the confidence and limits of that information.

## Do not

- Do not state current product facts from memory when they can be checked.
- Do not treat a product's marketing page as independent evidence of performance.
- Do not let an out-of-date configuration be reported as current without a rerun (see change control).
