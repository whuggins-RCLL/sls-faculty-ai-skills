# Stanford lens

Apply these considerations when a pilot touches institutional mission, responsibility, evidence, data, teaching, research, or equity. This is original faculty guidance, not binding Stanford policy, and it does not present itself as SLS-specific rules.

## Lenses

- **Mission** — Does the pilot serve legitimate teaching, scholarship, or administrative purposes?
- **Responsibility** — Who is accountable for outputs and actions during and after the pilot?
- **Evidence** — Are conclusions grounded in documented results rather than impressions?
- **Data and privacy** — Is the minimum necessary data used, with appropriate classification and handling?
- **Teaching and research** — Are students and scholarly integrity protected?
- **Equity and accessibility** — Does the pilot avoid excluding or disadvantaging anyone?

## Current-source rule

When a pilot depends on current facts, consult current official Stanford sources for:

- Available AI services
- Product availability and tiers
- Data-classification guidance
- Privacy or security guidance
- Agentic-AI guidance
- Teaching guidance
- Institutional review requirements
- Accessibility guidance

Record the research-checked date. Do not infer approval or prohibition from the absence of a public listing, and do not describe general Stanford University guidance as SLS-specific.

## Boundary

This Skill does not grant institutional approval, authorize data use, or certify compliance. When the decision requires those, name them as authorized-review steps for the appropriate offices.
