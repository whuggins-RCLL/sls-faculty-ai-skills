# Stop, kill, and escalation criteria

Every full pilot defines, in advance, the conditions under which it pauses, stops, escalates, or resumes. These protect people and data while the pilot is running.

## Four conditions to define

- **Pause** — halt temporarily, investigate, then decide.
- **Stop or kill** — end the pilot; the use is not continued under current conditions.
- **Escalate** — notify a named responsible role (and, where required, institutional review).
- **Resume** — the specific conditions under which a paused pilot may continue.

## Candidate triggers

- Restricted-data exposure
- Fabricated authority reaching a real work product
- Unauthorized action by an agent
- Missing or unusable audit trail
- Repeated critical failure
- Unmanageable review burden
- Accessibility barrier that excludes a participant
- Uneven-outcome pattern across groups
- Vendor-terms change affecting data handling
- Product or model change mid-pilot
- Unclear or changed data retention
- User confusion about the system's authority or role

## Write them concretely

Bad: "Stop if things go wrong." Good: "Stop immediately if any output places a fabricated citation into a document shared outside the pilot team, and escalate to the pilot lead within one business day."

## Preserve stopping power

The people who can pause or stop the pilot must be named, reachable, and empowered to act without waiting for the tool's or vendor's agreement. Governance design for authority and stopping power routes to the SLS AI Governance and Human Judgment Simulator.
