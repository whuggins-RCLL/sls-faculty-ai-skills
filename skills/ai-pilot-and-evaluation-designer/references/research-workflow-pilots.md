# Research workflow pilots

Pilots of AI in faculty research must protect source integrity, unpublished data, and scholarly accountability.

## Define the research decision

State the decision, for example: whether to use a source-grounded assistant for a first pass over a defined corpus; whether a summarization workflow is reliable enough to triage documents before human reading.

## Source and citation integrity

- Test for fabricated or non-existent sources.
- Test proposition support: does the cited source actually say what the output claims?
- Test for omitted counterevidence and adverse authority.
- Require traceable quotations; untraceable quotations are a critical failure.

## Unpublished and confidential data

- Prefer public or synthetic corpora for the pilot.
- Do not upload unpublished interviews, confidential datasets, or embargoed material without authorized approval and verified data handling.
- Confirm whether inputs are retained or used for training before any real research data is considered.

## Reproducibility

- Record configuration and dates; research conclusions must be reproducible.
- Test variability: does the workflow yield consistent results on the same inputs?

## Accountability

The faculty member remains the scholar of record. The pilot measures whether the workflow supports their judgment, not whether it can replace verification.

## Handoff

Verifying specific bibliographic or empirical claims is a claims question; route to the SLS AI Claims Cross-Examiner. Scholarly literature search and citation verification tools remain the researcher's responsibility.
