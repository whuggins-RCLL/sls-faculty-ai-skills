# Pilot plan template

Use this structure for a full pilot protocol. Fill every section; mark unknowns as open questions rather than guessing.

## Full pilot protocol

1. **Problem and baseline** — the real problem, the current workflow, and the baseline to compare against.
2. **Decision supported** — the concrete decision and the options in front of the decision-maker.
3. **Pilot question** — the filled-in question template.
4. **System identity** — product, model, feature, tier, region, tools, retrieval configuration, instructions, settings, account type, test and research dates.
5. **Participants and roles** — operator, reviewers, decision-maker, and who holds stopping power.
6. **Test-set design** — categories, counts, sourcing, and how difficult and boundary cases are included.
7. **Reference-answer method** — objective, expert, rubric, paired, blind, or consensus.
8. **Metrics** — the few metrics tied to the decision.
9. **Predetermined thresholds** — minimum acceptable, target, and unacceptable, set before results.
10. **Failure taxonomy** — critical, major, minor, cosmetic; and the critical-failure rules.
11. **Human review** — who reviews, time, detectability, and the verification-cost check.
12. **Data and permissions** — data required, sensitivity, least privilege, and open institutional questions.
13. **Accessibility and equity** — cases and checks; alternative workflow.
14. **Operations** — duration, scope, schedule, storage, administration.
15. **Logging** — what is recorded and how integrity is preserved.
16. **Change control** — the regression subset and rerun triggers.
17. **Stop and escalation criteria** — pause, stop, escalate, resume.
18. **Analysis plan** — how results are read against predetermined criteria; denominators; what the sample can establish.
19. **Limitations** — what the pilot cannot establish.
20. **Decision options** — the labeled outcomes the evidence could support.

## Rapid pilot (short form)

For lower-stakes decisions, a rapid pilot may compress sections 4–19 but must still include: the decision, a baseline, 8–15 representative cases including difficult ones, predetermined thresholds, critical-failure rules, a review method, data limits, and a stop condition.
