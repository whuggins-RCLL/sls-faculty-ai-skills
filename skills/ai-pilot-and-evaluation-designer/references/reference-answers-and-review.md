# Reference answers and review methods

How outputs are judged determines whether results mean anything. Define the review method before running the pilot.

## Reference and review methods

- Objective reference answer (a known-correct result)
- Expert-reviewed answer
- Criteria-based rubric (see the scoring rubric template)
- Paired comparison (A/B against baseline)
- Blind review (product identity hidden)
- Consensus review (multiple reviewers reconcile)

Choose the method the decision can rely on. Objective references suit factual tasks; rubrics suit judgment-heavy tasks; paired blind comparison reduces brand bias.

## Record for the review

- Reviewer qualifications
- Review instructions
- Disagreement procedure
- Whether product identity is hidden
- Review time per output
- Reviewer confidence
- Known limitations of the review

## Blinding

When feasible, hide which output came from which system (including the baseline). Brand and interface polish bias reviewers toward the AI output.

## Reviewer agreement

For rubric or judgment reviews, use at least two reviewers on a subset and record agreement and how disagreements are resolved. Low agreement is itself a finding about the task's measurability.

## Do not

- Do not describe unreviewed model output as a gold standard.
- Do not let the same person write the prompt, run the system, and be the sole judge of quality.
- Do not treat reviewer satisfaction as proof of accuracy.
