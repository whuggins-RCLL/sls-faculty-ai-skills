# Fictional scenario safeguards

Illustrative scenarios help faculty picture a pilot. Keep them clearly fictional and never attributed to real people or internal proposals.

## Rules

- Use fictional composite instructional scenarios only.
- Label them clearly as fictional (for example, "Illustrative scenario").
- Do not attribute scenarios to the Dean, named SLS leaders, named faculty, named students, current committees, or recognizable internal proposals.
- Invent neutral placeholders (for example, "a seminar instructor," "a clinic supervisor," "Vendor A").
- Do not imply that a fictional pilot reflects an actual Stanford decision, endorsement, or plan.

## Fictional legal materials

- Clearly label any fictional statute, case, or authority used as a test input.
- Never present a fabricated authority as real, and never let one leave the pilot as if it were genuine (that is a critical failure in results, and it must not happen in examples either).

## Purpose limit

Fictional scenarios illustrate method. They are not evidence. Do not draw conclusions about a real product from an invented example.
