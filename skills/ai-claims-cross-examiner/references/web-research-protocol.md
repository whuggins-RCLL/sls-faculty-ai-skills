# Web research protocol

## When live research is mandatory

Current product features; current models; benchmarks; pricing; vendor policies; Stanford guidance; recent studies; legal doctrine; regulations; market adoption; institutional practice; predictions based on recent developments; named research papers; named datasets; named reports; statements made after packaging.

Do not evaluate current evidence from model memory alone.

## Source priority

1. Official Stanford sources for Stanford-specific claims
2. Primary legal authorities for legal claims
3. Original research papers
4. Official technical documentation and model cards
5. Government or standards-body sources
6. Independent evaluations with transparent methods
7. Systematic reviews or strong scholarly synthesis
8. Vendor documentation for vendor-controlled facts
9. Credible independent reporting
10. User-provided material, clearly labeled
11. Stable background knowledge for general concepts only

## Do not rely primarily on

Search snippets; AI-generated summaries; SEO content; undisclosed sponsored content; headlines without underlying source; vendor claims as independent evidence; social-media virality; anonymous screenshots without context.

## Research freshness record

Every current examination should state:

- Research checked: [date]
- Claim source date: [when known]
- System, model, or product version examined: [when relevant]
- Stanford public-information status checked: [when relevant]
- Current legal authorities checked through: [date, when relevant]

## No web access

Limit to stable conceptual framing. State what research would be needed. Do not provide current evidence conclusions.

## Opening sources

For named papers, benchmarks, or news: open the original source. Do not rely on headline alone.
