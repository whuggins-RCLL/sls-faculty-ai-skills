# Predictions and future claims

For predictions about replacement, transformation, adoption, or risk:

Identify:

- Time horizon
- Defined outcome
- Assumed technology trajectory
- Adoption, cost, regulatory, organizational, human-behavior, infrastructure assumptions
- Countervailing forces
- Indicators to monitor

Use scenario ranges rather than false precision when appropriate.

Classify prediction confidence separately from current factual confidence.

Do not treat a confident speaker as evidence of a likely future.

## Tasks versus occupations

Occupations are bundles of tasks, responsibilities, relationships, authority, and institutional roles. Ask which tasks may change and who remains accountable.

## Provisional label

Often: Prediction with substantial uncertainty.

## Both sides of replacement claims

“AI will replace lawyers” and “AI will never replace lawyers” both require scope narrowing, task analysis, and assumption mapping. Neither should receive categorical acceptance or dismissal without evidence.
