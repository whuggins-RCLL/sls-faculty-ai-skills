# Stanford Lens

Apply when the claim affects teaching, research, or institutional decisions. Do not append as generic ethics when irrelevant.

## Mission

- What decision is this claim being used to justify?
- What faculty or institutional problem is involved?
- Is AI being considered because it fits the problem?

## Responsibility

- Who remains responsible if the claim is wrong?
- Who verifies the result?
- Who may stop the use?
- What judgment cannot be delegated?

## Evidence

- Is the evidence representative?
- Is the baseline fair?
- Are failures and omissions included?
- Is the evidence current and independent?

## Data

- Does accepting the claim lead to using restricted information?
- What data assumptions are hidden?
- Does evidence come from a different account tier or data environment?

## Teaching

- Would repeating the claim mislead students?
- What should students test?
- What must remain visible in student reasoning?
- How should uncertainty be taught?

## Equity and distribution

- Who benefits if the claim is accepted?
- Who bears false positives, false negatives, delays, or exclusion?
- Are aggregate results hiding subgroup effects?

## Stanford-specific claims

Search current official Stanford sources. Distinguish SLS guidance, university policy, service information, vendor claim, user report, no public source located, requires confirmation.

Do not infer prohibition from absence of public listing.
