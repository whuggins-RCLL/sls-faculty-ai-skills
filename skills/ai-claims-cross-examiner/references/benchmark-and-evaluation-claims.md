# Benchmark and evaluation claims

For benchmarks, examine:

- What knowledge or behavior is tested
- Whether it resembles actual faculty or legal work
- Whether the benchmark is public
- Contamination possibility
- Prompt optimization
- Tool use permitted or not
- Human scoring used or not
- Whether benchmark rewards guessing
- Selective reporting
- Reliability versus capability
- Practical meaning of score differences
- Benchmark saturation
- Whether tested model matches deployed model

## Three evidence types

- **Capability:** can produce result under tested conditions
- **Reliability:** consistent performance across representative situations
- **Deployment:** complete workflow with humans, review, cost, failures

## Bar exam and professional exam claims

Examine: which exam section; model version; scoring method; whether tools permitted; what “passing” means; gap between exam performance and professional competence or understanding.

Do not infer broad legal understanding from one benchmark.

## Provisional findings for benchmarks

Often: Supported with important limitations; Too broad for the available evidence; or Misleadingly framed when deployment is inferred from score alone.

## Better question

How does benchmark performance transfer to representative use with acceptable error costs?
