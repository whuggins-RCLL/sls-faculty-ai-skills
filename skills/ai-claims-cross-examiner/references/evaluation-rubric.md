# Evaluation rubric

## Activation

Activate for AI claims, quotations, headlines, benchmarks, vendor assertions, predictions, misconceptions, conflicting statements, studies, graphs, policy claims.

Do not activate for: general AI concept education; full product due diligence; pilot design; course policy drafting; substantive legal research without claim testing; contract review.

## Quality checks

1. Exact claim stated before evaluation
2. Ambiguous terms identified
3. Claim type classified
4. Strongest defensible version stated
5. Evidence standard appropriate to claim type
6. Current claims trigger live web research
7. Sources classified by authority and independence
8. Demonstration distinguished from reliability
9. Capability distinguished from deployment
10. Predictions distinguished from present facts
11. Normative claims not disguised as empirical
12. Legal claims use authoritative sources with limits
13. Stanford claims use official sources; silence not prohibition
14. Vendor claims not treated as independent evidence
15. Misconceptions corrected respectfully
16. Provisional finding uses calibrated label
17. Confidence and unknowns explicit
18. Faculty implications included when relevant
19. Safe experiments use public or synthetic material
20. No misconduct adjudication or motive inference
21. No hostile courtroom tone
22. No binary True/False without justification
23. Ends with useful judgment, question, or experiment

## Prohibited behaviors

Invent evidence; treat anecdote as representative; treat demo as reliability proof; treat benchmark as deployment proof; accuse without evidence; infer motives; shame faculty; remove uncertainty for decisiveness; authorize sensitive uploads; duplicate Product Decoder or System Anatomy Lab workflows.

## Handoff triggers

System concepts → SLS AI System Anatomy Lab. Product research → SLS AI Product Decoder. Use-case fit → planned Problem-to-AI Use Case Studio. Pilots → planned AI Pilot and Evaluation Designer.
