# Causal and comparative claims

## Causal claims

Examples: AI causes deskilling; AI improves learning; AI reduces bias; AI increases access to justice.

Ask:

- Is evidence causal or correlational?
- What alternative explanations exist?
- Was there a control or comparison group?
- How was outcome measured?
- Over what period?
- Who was affected?
- Were distributional effects measured?
- Is proposed mechanism supported?
- Could selection or novelty effects explain the result?

Do not imply lack of causal proof means the concern is irrelevant.

## Comparative claims

Examples: AI is better than lawyers; AI outperforms students; Product A beats Product B.

Require:

- Which AI system and human group
- Expertise level
- Task, time, materials, tool access
- Output format and evaluation standard
- Review process and cost
- Error severity
- Model version
- Whether humans could also use AI

Avoid category-wide conclusions from one narrow task.

## Competing studies

Explain methodological differences. Do not average casually. Identify what would resolve disagreement.
