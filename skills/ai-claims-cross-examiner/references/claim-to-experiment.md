# Claim to experiment

Convert disputed claims into safe experiments using public or synthetic material.

## Smallest Useful Test structure

**Claim being tested:** exact narrow claim

**What success would mean:** observable criterion

**What failure would mean:** observable criterion

**Materials:** public or synthetic

**Comparison:** baseline or alternative

**Sample:** enough examples to learn without overstating certainty

**Measure:** accuracy, omission, source support, variability, time, review burden, error severity, user experience

**Confounding factors:** list

**Stop condition:** failure or risk threshold

**What this test cannot establish:** limits

## Rules

Do not recommend sensitive, confidential, privileged, student-record, or restricted material.

Do not imply a small faculty experiment proves general validity.

## Examples of narrow tests

- Verify five citations from an AI legal research answer against primary sources
- Compare time-to-first-draft with and without AI on a synthetic memo task, including review time
- Test whether retrieval reduces unsupported factual claims on a fixed set of public questions
- Run the same prompt three times and record variability

## Handoff

When formal pilot or evaluation design is needed, route to planned AI Pilot and Evaluation Designer.
