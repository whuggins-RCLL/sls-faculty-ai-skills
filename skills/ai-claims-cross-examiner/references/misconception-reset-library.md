# Misconception reset library

Use Misconception Reset mode. Structure: The Claim; What Is Partly True; What Is Misleading; Better Mental Model; Evidence Status; Question to Ask Next.

## 1. AI is just autocomplete

Partly true: language models predict likely continuations.

Misleading: conceals learned representations, tool use, complex behavior, system layers.

Better question: Which capabilities matter for the task, and how reliably are they demonstrated?

## 2. AI can reason like a human

Partly true: models may produce multi-step solutions and useful analysis.

Misleading: human-like language does not establish human mental processes, stable understanding, or general reliability.

Better question: What reasoning behavior is tested, and how does it fail?

## 3. AI cannot reason

Partly true: models may fail unpredictably or imitate patterns.

Misleading: ignores tasks where systems produce successful multi-step inference.

Better question: What operational definition of reasoning is used?

## 4. AI always hallucinates

Partly true: generative systems can produce unsupported content.

Misleading: error rates vary across tasks, systems, sources, configurations.

Better question: What failure rate and error type are acceptable for this use?

## 5. Retrieval eliminates hallucinations

Partly true: retrieval can improve source access and grounding.

Misleading: system may misread, omit, combine, or misattribute retrieved material.

Better question: How are attribution, completeness, and reasoning evaluated?

## 6. Citations prove reliability

Partly true: citations can make verification easier.

Misleading: citation may not exist, may not support proposition, may omit contrary authority.

Better question: Was the proposition checked against the source?

## 7. Open-weight means private

Partly true: open weights may permit local hosting.

Misleading: privacy depends on deployment, access, logging, retention, security, operations.

Better question: Where does data flow, and who controls each layer?

## 8. Enterprise means safe

Partly true: enterprise tiers often add contractual and administrative controls.

Misleading: suitability depends on specific account, configuration, data, task, requirements.

Better question: Which protections apply to this exact use?

## 9. AI detectors can prove student misconduct

Partly true: some systems estimate patterns associated with generated text.

Misleading: probabilistic detection does not independently establish authorship or misconduct.

Better question: What independent evidence and institutional process would be required?

## 10. AI is more accurate than lawyers

Partly true: systems may outperform selected human groups on selected tasks.

Misleading: hides task, expertise, comparator, error severity, review conditions.

Better question: Which system, lawyers, task, and success standard?

## 11. AI will replace lawyers

Partly true: AI may automate or change particular tasks.

Misleading: occupations are bundles of tasks, responsibilities, relationships, authority, roles.

Better question: Which tasks may change, and who remains accountable?

## 12. AI will not replace lawyers

Partly true: professional judgment, authority, relationships remain important.

Misleading: categorical wording may ignore staffing, workflow, pricing, entry-level work changes.

Better question: Which parts of legal work are most exposed to change?

## 13. Bigger models are always better

Partly true: scaling may improve many capabilities.

Misleading: task fit, latency, cost, privacy, reliability, deployment constraints matter.

Better question: Better for which task and metric?

## 14. A benchmark proves real-world value

Partly true: benchmarks provide standardized evidence.

Misleading: may not represent actual workflows or failure costs.

Better question: How does benchmark performance transfer to representative use?

## 15. More automation means more efficiency

Partly true: automation may reduce direct task time.

Misleading: review, correction, monitoring, implementation, escalation may add work.

Better question: What is the total workflow cost?

## 16. No training means no data risk

Partly true: excluding customer data from training addresses one concern.

Misleading: retention, logging, human review, subprocessors, access, product improvement remain separate.

Better question: What happens to data across its full lifecycle?

## 17. Human in the loop makes AI safe

Partly true: human review may catch failures.

Misleading: reviewer needs time, expertise, authority, information, accountability.

Better question: Can the human realistically detect and correct relevant errors?

## 18. A polished answer is a good answer

Partly true: clear communication has value.

Misleading: fluency may conceal missing evidence, false authority, incorrect reasoning.

Better question: What independent signals support the answer?
