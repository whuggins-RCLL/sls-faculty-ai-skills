# Visuals and analogies

Every visual must have a text equivalent.

- Claim anatomy map

## Analogy cards

Each analogy card includes: analogy; what it explains; where it breaks.

### Claim as a contract clause

Analogy: A broad AI claim can be examined like a clause whose defined terms, conditions, exceptions, and remedies were omitted.

Explains: Small wording choices such as can, usually, reliably, and always create different commitments.

Breaks: Empirical claims also require evidence, not textual analysis alone.

### Demonstration versus evidence

Analogy: Showroom demonstration compared with road test across ordinary and difficult conditions.

Explains: Selected success proves possibility, not representative reliability.

Breaks: AI performance may change across prompts, models, sources, and time.

### Benchmark

Analogy: One examination measuring selected subjects rather than complete professional competence.

Explains: A score may provide useful standardized evidence without representing all real work.

Breaks: Benchmarks may be contaminated, optimized against, or scored unlike human examinations.

### Confidence

Analogy: Weather forecast with stated probability rather than a promise.

Explains: Calibrated conclusion can guide action without claiming certainty.

Breaks: AI claims may involve poorly specified outcomes without enough historical data for formal probabilities.
