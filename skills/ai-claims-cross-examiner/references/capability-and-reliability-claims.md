# Capability and reliability claims

## Capability claims

What can the system do, on which task, under which conditions, with which model or product version?

Capability evidence shows possibility under tested conditions. A demonstration proves selected success, not representative reliability.

## Reliability claims

How often does it succeed, compared with what standard, with what error types and severity?

Reliability requires repeated testing across representative situations, including failures and omissions.

## Common confusions

- Fluent output ≠ correct output
- One success ≠ consistent performance
- Benchmark score ≠ deployment readiness
- Retrieval access ≠ correct attribution or completeness
- Human in the loop ≠ safe if reviewer lacks time, expertise, or authority
- Citations present ≠ propositions supported

## Accuracy and hallucination

Error rates vary by task, system, sources, and configuration. Ask what failure rate and error type are acceptable for the intended use.

Retrieval can improve source access but may not eliminate misread, omission, combination, or misattribution errors.

## Enterprise and open-weight

Enterprise tiers may add contractual controls; suitability depends on account, configuration, data, task, and institutional requirements.

Open-weight may permit local hosting; privacy depends on deployment, access, logging, retention, security, and operations.
