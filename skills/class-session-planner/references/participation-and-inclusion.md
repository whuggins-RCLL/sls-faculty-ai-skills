# Participation and Inclusion

Avoid relying entirely on the same confident speakers. Options include individual reflection before discussion, written responses, anonymous polling, pair discussion, rotating roles, structured small groups, advance notice of complex questions, multiple ways to participate, clear cold-call expectations, accessible materials, and planned wait time.

Flag unnecessary barriers such as images without explanation, dense unstructured handouts, rapid-fire questioning, activities dependent on visual cues alone, uncaptioned media, unexplained jargon, inaccessible tables, and participation technologies that disadvantage some students. Do not claim formal accessibility compliance.
