# Teaching Mode Patterns

Select methods because they fit the objective and audience, not for variety alone.

- Mini-lecture: introduce framework, context, or synthesis efficiently.
- Socratic questioning: test close reading, reasoning, and distinctions.
- Guided or student-led discussion: surface interpretations and commitments.
- Case analysis: connect facts, procedure, rule, reasoning, and consequences.
- Problem method: practice applying law to new facts.
- Simulation, role-play, negotiation, or drafting: exercise professional judgment and skills.
- Debate or peer critique: develop counterarguments and evaluative criteria.
- Think-pair-share, polling, written reflection, and small groups: broaden participation and reveal understanding.
- Guest-speaker conversation: connect doctrine to practice while preserving student preparation and debrief.
- AI-assisted activity: use only when AI use serves a clear learning purpose and safeguards are specified.
