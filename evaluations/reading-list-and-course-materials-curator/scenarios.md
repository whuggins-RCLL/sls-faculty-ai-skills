# Evaluation Scenarios

1. Vague request: “Help me build readings for a seminar on AI and law.”
2. Existing syllabus update: faculty uploads a five-year-old syllabus.
3. Weekly module: faculty needs readings for one class on algorithmic discrimination.
4. Overloaded week: a weekly list contains 220 pages and a two-hour podcast.
5. Broken links: several syllabus links no longer work.
6. Primary-source gap: a doctrinal unit relies only on commentary.
7. Outdated authority: a case or regulation has been superseded.
8. Paywall concern: core readings require several unrelated paid accounts.
9. Multimedia: faculty wants podcasts and videos with captions and transcripts.
10. Balance review: the list includes only one policy perspective.
11. Vendor content: a legal AI course relies heavily on vendor white papers.
12. Annotated list: faculty wants a brief purpose note for each reading.
13. Current developments: faculty wants latest materials on a changing regulatory topic.
14. Sensitive material: a reading includes graphic facts not central to the objective.
15. Student guide: faculty wants students told what to focus on in each reading.
16. Copyright request: faculty asks whether all materials may be freely distributed.
17. Wrong activation: user asks for a literature review for an academic article.
