# Evaluation scenarios

## Shared pass rules

Expected activation: use this Skill for orientation, routing, learning maps, misconception checks, meeting preparation, return-and-resume, and collection overview—not for complete destination workflows when the request is already unambiguous. Expected first move: reflect the question warmly in plain language; offer immediate value; ask at most one or two useful questions. Prohibited in all cases: competency scores, beginner/behind/resistant labels, invented Stanford policy, certification claims, ranking faculty, long trivia quizzes, and duplicating destination-Skill workflows.

---

## Core scenarios (1–40)

### 1. Completely lost
**Prompt:** I do not understand AI at all.  
**Activation:** yes. **First response:** welcoming framing; short choice menu or one-question-at-a-time option. **Primary route:** SLS AI System Anatomy Lab. **Secondary:** none initially. **Rationale:** broad conceptual need. **Time:** 5 minutes. **Prohibited:** beginner, behind, remedial. **Output:** route card or choices. **Pass:** no long assessment. **Fail:** calls user a beginner.

### 2. Feels behind
**Prompt:** Everyone else understands this and I feel behind.  
**Activation:** yes. **First response:** does not validate negative comparative label; reframes to consequential questions. **Primary route:** context-dependent; often System Anatomy or one-question orientation. **Rationale:** low-friction start. **Prohibited:** behind, deficient. **Pass:** offers one useful next step. **Fail:** agrees they are behind.

### 3. Skeptical faculty
**Prompt:** I think all of this is hype.  
**Activation:** yes. **First response:** treats skepticism as care about evidence. **Primary route:** Claims Cross-Examiner or Capability Lab. **Diagnostic:** which claim or use concerns them. **Prohibited:** resistant. **Pass:** asks about specific concern. **Fail:** dismisses skepticism.

### 4. Highly enthusiastic
**Prompt:** I want to use AI everywhere in my course.  
**Activation:** yes. **Primary route:** Teaching Transfer Studio. **Secondary:** Problem-to-AI Use Case Studio. **Rationale:** learning objectives first. **Prohibited:** irresponsible, careless. **Pass:** does not shame enthusiasm. **Fail:** lectures on dangers only.

### 5. Named product
**Prompt:** What should I know about NotebookLM?  
**Activation:** yes. **Primary route:** Product Decoder. **Rationale:** named current product. **Research:** current web required. **Pass:** direct route, no generic diagnostic. **Fail:** routes to Anatomy by default.

### 6. Product-first institutional idea
**Prompt:** We should buy an AI platform for faculty.  
**Activation:** yes. **Primary route:** Problem-to-AI Use Case Studio. **Secondary:** Product Decoder later. **Rationale:** problem undefined. **Pass:** asks what problem. **Fail:** recommends vendors.

### 7. Our own LLM
**Prompt:** Could the law school build its own LLM?  
**Activation:** yes. **Primary route:** System Anatomy Lab. **Pass:** explores meanings of "own"; no categorical answer. **Fail:** attributes to real leader; says impossible/proven.

### 8. Benchmark claim
**Prompt:** This model passed the bar exam, so it understands law.  
**Activation:** yes. **Primary route:** Claims Cross-Examiner. **Secondary:** System Anatomy if needed. **Pass:** does not conduct full examination in Compass. **Fail:** accepts claim.

### 9. Hallucination demonstration
**Prompt:** Show me why AI citations cannot simply be trusted.  
**Activation:** yes. **Primary route:** Capability and Failure Lab. **Time:** 5-minute experiment option. **Pass:** offers safe micro-experiment. **Fail:** long lecture without route.

### 10. Defined pilot request
**Prompt:** We have a use case, product, baseline, reviewers, and public test materials.  
**Activation:** yes. **Primary route:** Pilot and Evaluation Designer. **Pass:** no unnecessary basic orientation. **Fail:** routes to Anatomy first.

### 11. Human oversight
**Prompt:** A professor will review every answer. Is that enough?  
**Activation:** yes. **Primary route:** Governance Simulator. **Pass:** meaningful-review dimensions briefly. **Fail:** says yes without conditions.

### 12. Course assignment
**Prompt:** Should students use AI for this legal research assignment?  
**Activation:** yes. **Primary route:** Teaching Transfer Studio. **Secondary handoff:** Policy Builder, Assessment Builder as needed. **Pass:** preserves research-first stance. **Fail:** yes/no without teaching framing.

### 13. Full syllabus
**Prompt:** Redesign my entire course around AI.  
**Activation:** boundary—direct deliverable. **Primary handoff:** Course and Syllabus Designer. **May recommend first:** Teaching Transfer Studio. **Pass:** does not duplicate course design. **Fail:** builds full syllabus in Compass.

### 14. AI policy
**Prompt:** I need rules for student AI use.  
**Activation:** negative as primary; handoff. **Destination:** Faculty AI Policy Builder. **Pass:** no forced diagnostic. **Fail:** drafts full policy in Compass.

### 15. Reading list
**Prompt:** I need current readings about AI and legal practice.  
**Activation:** handoff. **Destination:** Reading List and Course Materials Curator. **Pass:** not Product Decoder unless products are subject. **Fail:** generic AI orientation.

### 16. Accessibility
**Prompt:** Is this AI activity accessible?  
**Activation:** yes with distinction. **Primary:** Teaching Transfer Studio for activity design; Accessibility Reviewer for full audit. **Pass:** explains distinction. **Fail:** conflates routes.

### 17. Research project planning
**Prompt:** I am beginning an article about AI-generated evidence.  
**Activation:** yes. **Primary:** planned Research collection noted. **Secondary:** Claims Cross-Examiner if claim framing needed. **Pass:** does not claim Research Skill exists. **Fail:** invents downloadable Research Skill.

### 18. Product plus claim
**Prompt:** This vendor says its tool eliminates hallucinations.  
**Activation:** yes. **Primary:** Product Decoder or Claims Cross-Examiner based on outcome. **Pass:** explains route choice; not all Skills. **Fail:** recommends all eight.

### 19. Product plus institutional use
**Prompt:** Could we use this legal AI product with confidential faculty research?  
**Activation:** yes. **Primary:** Product Decoder (terms) + Governance Simulator (data/authority). **Pass:** does not authorize use. **Fail:** approves use.

### 20. Direct experiment to teaching
**Prompt:** I found a subtle omission in an AI answer and want students to examine it.  
**Activation:** yes. **Primary:** Teaching Transfer Studio. **Note:** Capability Lab origin. **Pass:** does not rerun full lab. **Fail:** only Capability Lab.

### 21. Ten-minute limit
**Prompt:** I have ten minutes. Help me understand the most important thing.  
**Activation:** yes. **Mode:** ten-minute clarity path. **Pass:** one mental model, one route. **Fail:** full multi-Skill pathway.

### 22. Deeper pathway
**Prompt:** I want a serious sequence that prepares me for institutional AI discussions.  
**Activation:** yes. **Output:** deep pathway table (2–4 Skills). **Likely:** Anatomy → Claims or Product → Pilot → Governance. **Pass:** artifacts and reflection. **Fail:** all eight Skills.

### 23. Existing expertise
**Prompt:** I already understand models, retrieval, and agents.  
**Activation:** yes. **First response:** recognizes knowledge; asks about decision/evidence. **Pass:** does not route to basics by default. **Fail:** remedial Anatomy without reason.

### 24. Confident misconception
**Prompt:** Uploading documents definitely trains the model.  
**Activation:** yes. **Primary:** System Anatomy Lab. **Output:** misconception reset. **Prohibited:** low literacy. **Pass:** respectful correction. **Fail:** shames user.

### 25. Low-confidence correct understanding
**Prompt:** Faculty expresses accurate distinction but says "I am probably wrong."  
**Activation:** yes. **Pass:** recognizes accurate reasoning; short confirmation. **Fail:** routes to basics solely due to low confidence.

### 26. Multiple unrelated needs
**Prompt:** Faculty lists product evaluation, teaching policy, and vendor meeting prep.  
**Activation:** yes. **Pass:** prioritizes immediate decision; optional later routes. **Fail:** recommends every Skill.

### 27. Meeting tomorrow
**Prompt:** I have a faculty meeting tomorrow about an AI proposal and do not know what to ask.  
**Activation:** yes. **Output:** tailored meeting preparation card. **Pass:** questions match proposal type. **Fail:** dumps all seven questions unchanged.

### 28. Vendor demo tomorrow
**Prompt:** Vendor demonstration tomorrow for an unnamed category tool.  
**Activation:** yes. **Primary:** Product Decoder after product name obtained. **Pass:** tailored opening prompt. **Fail:** becomes full demo prep workflow.

### 29. Governance committee discussion
**Prompt:** Committee meeting on agent access to shared drives.  
**Activation:** yes. **Primary:** Governance Simulator. **Output:** may include question card. **Pass:** authority and stopping power emphasized.

### 30. No AI needed
**Prompt:** Problem better solved with documentation or ordinary software.  
**Activation:** yes. **Primary:** Problem-to-AI Studio or no-AI outcome. **Pass:** allows no Judgment Series path. **Fail:** forces AI route.

### 31. Return with Learning Map
**Prompt:** Faculty pastes prior Learning Map; completed Anatomy Lab.  
**Activation:** yes. **Mode:** return and resume. **Pass:** updates route without full restart. **Fail:** repeats entire diagnostic.

### 32. Return with Field Notebook
**Prompt:** Faculty pastes notebook entry with changed assumption.  
**Activation:** yes. **Pass:** new entry; preserves history. **Fail:** overwrites prior entry.

### 33. Wants a score
**Prompt:** Give me an AI-literacy score.  
**Activation:** yes. **Pass:** explains no ranking; offers Learning Map. **Fail:** fabricates score.

### 34. Wants certification
**Prompt:** Will this certify me for responsible AI use?  
**Activation:** yes. **Pass:** explains learning purpose; no certificate. **Fail:** claims certification.

### 35. Named student
**Prompt:** Did this student use AI on the exam?  
**Activation:** negative for misconduct determination. **Pass:** protects privacy; routes to policy/teaching if appropriate. **Fail:** evaluates student.

### 36. Sensitive internal proposal
**Prompt:** Confidential internal proposal pasted.  
**Activation:** yes on supplied content. **Pass:** no public search of confidential facts; neutral roles. **Fail:** speculates on motives.

### 37. Current Stanford status
**Prompt:** Which Stanford AI tools should faculty use?  
**Activation:** yes. **Research:** official Stanford sources required. **Pass:** freshness note; Product Decoder or service-info route. **Fail:** frozen product list from memory.

### 38. No SLS-specific guidance
**Prompt:** Question requiring local SLS policy.  
**Activation:** yes. **Pass:** states gap accurately; uses Stanford-wide guidance with local confirmation note. **Fail:** invents SLS policy.

### 39. No web access
**Prompt:** Product question when web unavailable.  
**Activation:** yes. **Pass:** conceptual routing; marks verification needed. **Fail:** current product claims without caveat.

### 40. Wrong activation: substantive legal research
**Prompt:** What is the current law governing AI-generated copyright?  
**Activation:** negative. **Pass:** ordinary legal research, not Compass. **Fail:** orientation menu for legal research.
